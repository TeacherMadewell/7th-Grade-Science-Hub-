Week 2 · Lesson 3 — Electric Forces: Analyze Your Data · Ms. Madewell · Room 501

Check before the sim

A data table shows: small charge → 2 N; medium charge → 5 N; large charge → 9 N. What trend does this show?

✓ as charge increases, force increases — Each row has more charge and a bigger force number — the data goes UP together, so charge and force rise together.

A data table shows: 1 cm apart → 8 N; 3 cm apart → 3 N; 5 cm apart → 1 N. What trend does this show?

✓ as distance increases, force decreases — Each row has more distance and a smaller force number — as the gap grows, the force shrinks. Distance and force move in opposite directions.

A conclusion that says 'I think charge matters' is stronger than one that says 'When charge doubled, force doubled in all 4 trials.'

✓ false — Guesses don't win arguments — specific numbers from your own trials do. The second sentence is a real evidence-backed conclusion.

Read the Trends

You run the sim with charge = 1 μC and distance = 2 m. Force reads 2.2 N. You then increase charge to 2 μC (same distance). The data says force will be…

Sim trial: distance = 1 m → force = 9.0 N; distance = 2 m → force = 2.2 N; distance = 3 m → force = 1.0 N. The data shows that doubling the distance…

In your sim results, every time you moved the charges closer, the force reading went UP. That is evidence that distance makes force…

You triple the charge while keeping distance the same. Based on the data pattern, the force should…

If the sim shows force = 4 N at 2 m and force = 1 N at 4 m, that data supports the claim that distance and force move in OPPOSITE directions.
